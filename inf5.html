Atmosphere

The planet's cloud formations and wind patterns may be surprisingly different from those of Earth. Similar to what scientists suspect surrounded Mars when it was young. The model atmosphere contains a deep layer of clouds on the side facing the star. When starlight passes through the atmosphere of a planet, it interacts with molecules such as carbon dioxide and nitrogen to produce distinct signals called spectral lines.
